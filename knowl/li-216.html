Item 6.3.3.a.
Rewrite your model as below, where \(p\) remains the percentage of people vaccinated, and \(q\) is the efficacy percentage. We require that \(0 \leq p \leq 1\) and \(0 \leq q \leq 1\text{.}\) Explain in words why the way the model is written, and the ranges for \(p\) and \(q\text{,}\) make sense. In your explanation, include “edge cases”, that is, describe what happens when \(p=0\text{,}\) \(p=1\text{,}\) \(q=0\text{,}\) and \(q=1\text{.}\) Also describe what happens as \(p\) increases from \(0\) to \(1\text{,}\) and what happens as \(q\) increases from \(0\) to \(1\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \frac{dS}{dt} \amp = - (1-p)(1-q) \beta S I \\ \frac{dE}{dt} \amp = (1-p)(1-q) \beta S I - \kappa E \\ \frac{dI}{dt} \amp = \kappa E - \gamma I \\ \frac{dR}{dt} \amp = \gamma I. \end{align*}
