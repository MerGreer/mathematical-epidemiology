First, we should state a definition for \(p\text{.}\) We define \(p\) to be the fraction of people vaccinated against a disease, noting that the values of \(p\) are therefore restricted to \(0 \leq p \leq 1\text{,}\) where \(p=0\) means no one is vaccinated, and \(p=1\) means \(100\%\) of the population is vaccinated. Initially, we will think of vaccination as being \(100\%\) effective. 1  When the fraction \(p\) of people are vaccinated, the remaining fraction \((1-p)\) are not vaccinated.
