The book Mathematical Epidemiology
 1 
Mathematical Epidemiology was edited by Fred Brauer, Pauline van den Driessche, and Jianhong Wu, and published by Springer in 2008. The model we describe comes from Chapter 12, which was written by Fred Brauer, and is labeled SLIAR. Our version is labeled SEIAR to stay consistent with notation we have used in other chapters.
introduces a model of influenza having five compartments. In addition to Susceptibles (S), Infectious (I), and Removed (R), there is an Exposed-and-incubating (E) compartment for people incubating influenza who are not yet Infectious, and there is an Asymptomatic (A) compartment for people who are affected by influenza but do not show symptoms, yet are capable of spreading influenza to others. The compartmental diagram for this model appears in Figure 14.2.
Hint.
Only one compartment of the SIR model involves individuals experiencing some stage of infection, namely, the I compartment. This means that when we write the equations in matrix form, we use a \(1 \times 1\) matrix. Then \(\widehat{F}= \begin{pmatrix} \beta SI \end{pmatrix}\) and \(\widehat{V}= \begin{pmatrix} \gamma I \end{pmatrix}.\) Computing partial derivatives with respect to \(I\text{,}\) and evaluating at the disease-free equilibrium \((S, I, R)=(N, 0, 0)\) results in \(F = \begin{pmatrix} \beta N \end{pmatrix}\) and \(V = \begin{pmatrix} \gamma \end{pmatrix}.\) Computing \(FV^{-1}\) results in \(FV^{-1} = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{\beta N}{\gamma} \end{pmatrix}\text{,}\) and the one eigenvalue of \(FV^{-1}\) tells us that \(\mathcal{R}_0 = \frac{\beta N}{\gamma}\text{.}\)
