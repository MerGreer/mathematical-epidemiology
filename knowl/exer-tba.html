As we mentioned back in Chapter 2, a differential equation is just an equation in which at least one term is a derivative. This means that an equation like \(f'(t)=\cos t + 3\) is a differential equation.
Solving a differential equation often means finding a way to write an equivalent relationship, without including a derivative. Using this interpretation of “solving”: solve \(f'(t)=\cos t + 3\text{.}\)
