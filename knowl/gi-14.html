Parameters in a model are numbers, determined using biological information, that represent some aspect of how a disease progresses. In this text, most parameters are shown as Greek letters, such as \(\beta\) or \(\gamma\text{.}\) A few parameters in this text represent percentages, such as the percentage of a population that is vaccinated against a disease, and these parameters are typically represented by lowercase letters such as \(p\) or \(q\text{.}\) Parameters are different than variables: the variables in our models are typically the independent variable \(t\) for time, and dependent variables such as \(S\text{,}\) \(I\text{,}\) \(R\text{,}\) and others, for populations.
